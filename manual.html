Aircraft & Mission

The Heewing T2 is a fixed-wing tilt-rotor VTOL operating as an airborne relay carrier. Its mission is to extend the effective range and altitude of FPV sub-drones by acting as a relay node — bridging ground-station control and video links to one or more FPV aircraft operating beyond direct line-of-sight.

The carrier takes off vertically (VTOL), transitions to fixed-wing cruise, loiters at altitude over the FPV operating area, and relays both RC control (ELRS 868MHz → 2.4GHz) and FPV video (5.8GHz VTX → IP stream via SIYI HM30) back to the ground station.

VTOL Assist

VTOL assist activates automatically in fixed-wing modes when airspeed drops below 9 m/s or when altitude is below 25m AGL. If VTOL assist triggers unexpectedly during cruise, check for airspeed sensor failure or gusty conditions.

Tilt-Rotor Configuration

The T2 uses a front-pair tilting rotor design. The two front motors tilt forward to provide both VTOL lift and fixed-wing thrust — they are the primary propulsion in both flight modes. The two rear motors provide VTOL lift only and stop when the aircraft transitions to fixed-wing flight.

These 7 waypoints must be loaded and verified before every flight. The altitude profile below shows the intended flight path.

Do not wait for the 4s auto-abort. If transition looks wrong at 2–3s, switch QHOVER immediately. An aborted transition near transition altitude is recoverable — a stall is not.
Power-Up Sequence
Power up in this order every time. Reversing the sequence risks motor spin on unexpected link establishment.
1
SIYI HM30 ground unit

Power mast unit (6S 3000mAh). Confirm HM30 LED blue. Open MPV.net — no video expected yet.

2
FPV sub-drone

Power FPV. Confirm ELRS RX 2.4GHz connected. VTX begins transmitting 5.8GHz.

3
Carrier drone

Connect single 6S pack — all carrier systems power on together (FC, DBR4 relay TX, 5.8G VRX, CVBS→HDMI, SIYI chain). Wait for ArduPlane boot tone. ELRS 2.4GHz relay link establishes to FPV. SIYI air unit links to HM30. VRX acquires FPV signal → video appears in MPV.net.

4
ELRS 915MHz TX (last)

Power physical Nomad controller. ELRS 915MHz link to carrier establishes. Confirm in Mission Planner — RC inputs visible, no pre-arm errors.

Takeoff — VTOL Phase
1
Mode: QHOVER

Nomad switch position 1. Confirm in Mission Planner HUD.

2
Arm

Stick arm or GCS arm. All 4 motors spool to min arm thrust. Confirm no pre-arm messages.

3
Hover check at 2–3m

Confirm stable hover, no oscillation. Confirm SIYI link, ELRS RSSI acceptable. Co-pilot confirms telemetry nominal in MP.

4
Climb to transition altitude

Climb in QHOVER to ≥25m AGL. Wind vane active — aircraft weathervanes into wind automatically.

Transition — VTOL → Fixed Wing
Highest-risk phase. If pitch-down aggressive or airspeed does not build within 3s: revert to QHOVER immediately. Q_TRANS_FAIL auto-aborts at 4s — do not wait for it.
1
Switch to FBWB

Nomad switch position 2. Front tilt motors begin rotating forward. Cruise motor spools.

2
Monitor 3s transition window

Target 13 m/s. Minimum 11 m/s. If no acceleration: QHOVER immediately.

3
Transition confirmed

VTOL motors stop. Aircraft in fixed-wing flight. Co-pilot advances mission to WP2.

4
Switch to AUTO

Nomad switch position 3. Carrier Pilot takes over MP monitoring. Co-pilot clears to operate FPV.

Relay Operations
1
Loiter established — FPV handover

Carrier in AUTO loiter. Carrier Pilot confirms relay RSSI and video in MPV.net. Co-pilot takes FPV TX and begins operating sub-drone.

2
Carrier Pilot: orbit management

Primary duty — keep FPV inside relay bubble. Carrier orbits around the FPV operating area, FPV on the near side. If FPV approaches relay edge: call "RELAY EDGE" and vector carrier in via GUIDED mode.

3
Battery discipline

No BATT_LOW_VOLT configured — manual voltage monitoring only. Target RTL at 24.0V (~4.0V/cell). Hard limit: RTL before 22.8V. At 22.2V: automated Loiter→QLand at current position.

FPV pilot verbal callouts are the Carrier Pilot's only FPV status source. Establish a call cadence: battery level, position, issues — every 2–3 minutes minimum.
Recovery
1
Recall FPV

Co-pilot returns FPV sub-drone first where possible.

2
Carrier return

Mission WP5 → WP6 → WP7 (VTOL_LAND). Or manual RTL via Nomad left button.

3
Back-transition

10s window. Front motors tilt back to vertical. If oscillation appears: switch QHOVER manually.

4
VTOL land

Final descent at Q_LAND_FINAL_SPD = 0.2 m/s. Confirm landing zone clear. Disarm on touchdown.

Memorise these actions. In-flight response time is measured in seconds.
RC Link Loss — 915MHz (Ground → Carrier)
1
Short loss (≤1.5s): ArduPlane switches to FBWA automatically. Regain link and resume.
2
Long loss (>5s): RTL triggers at 120m AGL. Do NOT power-cycle TX in flight — reconnect on live link can cause RC spike.
3
SIYI HM30 GCS still available: send mode commands via Mission Planner. GCS failsafe only active in AUTO mode.
Relay Link Loss — 2.4GHz (Carrier → FPV)
1
FPV failsafe activates. Co-pilot ceases all inputs immediately.
2
Carrier Pilot holds carrier loiter — sub-drone may be RTH-ing toward carrier position.
3
If relay does not recover: co-pilot monitors FPV failsafe landing. Carrier Pilot recovers carrier independently.
Video Loss — 5.8GHz or SIYI Chain
1
Co-pilot: zero inputs on FPV TX. Sub-drone to hover/stabilise.
2
Carrier Pilot: descend carrier to reduce path loss. Wait 10 seconds.
3
Not recovered: co-pilot activates FPV RTH. Carrier Pilot recovers carrier independently.
4
Check: 5.8G VRX channel match, CVBS→HDMI converter power, SIYI air unit link to HM30.
Transition Failure — VTOL → FW
1
No airspeed build within 3s or excessive pitch-down: QHOVER immediately.
2
Q_TRANS_FAIL=4s — ArduPlane auto-aborts at 4s. Do not wait for automatic abort.
3
Land in VTOL. Inspect tilt servo travel, cruise motor ESC, battery voltage before next attempt.
Battery Critical — Carrier
1
No BATT_LOW_VOLT set. First automated action: 22.2V → Loiter to QLand at current position. Will NOT return home.
2
Initiate manual RTL at 24V. If dropping rapidly: Nomad left button → RTL immediately.
3
If Loiter→QLand triggers: confirm area below is clear. Aircraft descending vertically from current position.
GCS Link Loss (SIYI HM30)
1
Switch to physical ELRS 915MHz Nomad immediately. Confirm carrier responds to RC inputs.
2
In AUTO: GCS failsafe triggers RTL. In FBWB/QHOVER: no failsafe — fly manually with Nomad switch.
3
Mode changes via Nomad 3-position switch (QHOVER / FBWB / AUTO) or left button for RTL only. No MP available.

Role Overview

This system requires two trained operators working co-located with verbal communications only. No radio or earpiece between pilots — both must be within normal speaking distance throughout the sortie.

Neither role may be filled by an untrained operator. A solo Carrier Pilot sortie is permitted only for carrier-only test flights with no relay operations.

Carrier Pilot — Sign-Off Criteria
1
Solo VTOL check (3 flights)

3 consecutive flights: QHOVER arm → climb → FBWB transition → AUTO cruise → back-transition → VTOL_LAND. No aborts. Demonstrates battery management (manual RTL initiated at 24V or above).

2
Mission Planner proficiency

Successfully uploads a 7-waypoint mission, verifies waypoint altitudes, and sends a GCS mode command during an actual flight.

3
Emergency verbal recitation

Verbally walks through all 6 emergency scenarios with a qualified observer. No notes permitted. Observer confirms all actions are correct.

4
Supervised relay sortie

First relay operation conducted with a qualified observer present (not the FPV Pilot). Observer may not intervene except for safety.

Joint Crew — Pre-Mission Brief

Required before every sortie, regardless of crew experience level. Both pilots must agree on go/no-go before any equipment is powered.

1
Mission review

Both pilots walk through the uploaded mission: launch point, transition corridor, relay altitude, FPV operating area, return profile.

2
Weather go/no-go

Check weather tab. Both pilots agree. If either pilot is not satisfied — no flight.

3
Emergency refresh

Name the most likely emergency for today's conditions (e.g. wind = transition failure). Agree on abort criteria for the sortie.

4
Communication check

Confirm both pilots can hear each other clearly from their operating positions before committing to flight.

Communication Protocol

Both pilots are co-located at all times. All in-flight communication is direct verbal only — no radio, earpiece, or messaging during flight.

Brief
One to five words per call. No narration during active flight phases — save it for post-flight debrief.
Timely
Call when the condition occurs. Early warning is always preferable to a late report.
Unambiguous
Use the exact phrases listed below. Both pilots rehearse these before every new crew pairing.
Acknowledged
Recipient always acknowledges with "COPY" or by repeating the key word. Silence is not acceptance.
The FPV Pilot carries most of the in-flight callout burden. The Carrier Pilot's attention is on the carrier aircraft — FPV Pilot is the eyes for FPV sub-drone status.

Abort Calls
Either Pilot May Call an Abort

Any abort call is acted on immediately by the Carrier Pilot — no discussion in the air. Debrief after landing.

CalloutMeaning & immediate action
"ABORT ABORT ABORT"Full mission abort. Carrier Pilot: QHOVER if in VTOL phase, or RTL if in cruise. FPV Pilot: land FPV immediately using GPS Return.
"CARRIER RTL NOW"Carrier emergency RTL — Carrier Pilot activates by any means available (MP GCS or Nomad left button hold).
"FPV FAILSAFE"FPV GPS Rescue has activated. Carrier Pilot holds loiter. FPV Pilot monitors OSD — do not touch FPV TX sticks.
"CLEAR THE ZONE"Aircraft descending into landing zone — all personnel move clear immediately, no exceptions.

The mission must be planned and uploaded before arriving on site wherever possible. Review all items below before committing to a flight day.

ItemCheck

TerrainIdentify the highest terrain between the launch site, the relay loiter point, and the FPV operating area. Relay altitude must clear this by at least 30m.
Landing zoneIdentify and mark the exact VTOL landing point. Clear 5m radius, level, firm surface. GPS accuracy is ±2m — size the marked zone accordingly.

Relay Altitude & Orbit Geometry

The carrier must maintain RF line-of-sight simultaneously to the ground station and to the FPV operating area. Altitude is the primary tool.

Relay Altitude — Guide
Terrain between carrier and groundRecommended relay altitude
Open flat ground, distance <1km60–80m AGL
Low rolling terrain (<20m relief)80–100m AGL
Moderate terrain (20–50m relief)100–140m AGL
Significant terrain or urban (>50m obstacles)Requires site-specific analysis — do not estimate
The carrier→FPV link (2.4GHz) has less diffraction margin than 868MHz. If link budget is the constraint, increase relay altitude first before adjusting orbit radius.
Orbit Geometry
1
Orbit radius

150–250m loiter radius works well for relay geometry while allowing orbit adjustments. Smaller radii tighten turns and increase bank angle — reduce radius in calm conditions only.

2
Orbit position

Centre the orbit over the FPV operating area centroid. The carrier should have the FPV sub-drone on its near side at all times — keeps both RF links as short and direct as possible.

3
Avoid body-shadowing geometry

If the carrier passes directly between the ground station and the FPV aircraft, both RF links may experience a brief null. Plan orbit direction to minimise time in this geometry.

Transition Planning
1
Wind direction relative to WP2

Crosswind or slightly downwind is the preferred transition corridor. A headwind transition is fine — a tailwind transition requires more airspeed margin, so add 10m altitude to WP2.

2
Obstacle clearance

The corridor from WP1 to WP2 must be completely clear to at least 60m AGL. The aircraft gains altitude slowly during the VTOL→FW transition — this is the most vulnerable phase.

3
Q_TRANS_FAIL=4s abort window

If transition doesn't complete within 4s of AUTO reaching WP2, ArduPlane aborts to QHOVER. In strong headwind, the airspeed sensor may be slow to register — increase WP2 altitude to give more time inside the abort window.

4
Back-transition glide (Q_BACKTRANS_MS=10s)

The aircraft travels approximately 100–130m forward at cruise speed during the 10s back-transition. Plan WP5→WP6 to account for this before vertical VTOL descent begins at WP7.
